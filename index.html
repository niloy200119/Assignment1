Bangladesh Launches New Satellite

Bangladesh made a historic leap in space technology with the successful launch of its first satellite, Bangabandhu-1, on May 11, 2018. Named after the nation's founding father, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, the satellite was launched from the Kennedy Space Center in Florida using a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket. It is a geostationary communications satellite designed to improve telecommunications, broadcasting, and internet services across the country, including remote and rural areas. The project signifies Bangladesh's entry into the elite space club and showcases its ambition in science and technology. Operated by the Bangladesh Communication Satellite Company Limited (BCSCL), it also aids in disaster management and economic growth by fostering connectivity.
